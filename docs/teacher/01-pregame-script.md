# Star Bite Diner — Teacher pre-game script

**Total time:** ~5 minutes (3 min hook + rules, 1 min setup, 1 min priming questions)

Read or paraphrase. **Do NOT** mention "AI," "machine learning," "training data," "model," or "data poisoning" yet — those words are saved for the debrief, after students have lived the concepts.

---

## 1. Hook (60 seconds — read aloud or paraphrase)

> "Imagine you're running a restaurant — but every cook in your kitchen is a robot. And the robots have never made food before. They don't know what 'rare' means. They don't know which trash is recyclable. They don't know anything yet.
>
> It's your job — and the rest of your crew — to teach them. By giving them examples. Lots of them. 'This patty is rare.' 'This banana peel goes in compost.' One example at a time.
>
> But here's the catch: some people on your crew are SECRETLY saboteurs. They look exactly like everyone else. They go to the same training stations. They give labels just like you do. But they're trying to teach the robots **wrong** on purpose, to make customers angry and tank the restaurant.
>
> Your job is to train the bots fast and well — while figuring out who's helping and who's secretly sabotaging. The robots are only as good as the examples you give them. Train carefully."

---

## 2. Quick rules (2 minutes — talk through, don't read as a list)

Cover these in any order. Paraphrase, use your own words.

### Roles
- Most of you are **trainers**. Some of you are **saboteurs**.
- You'll find out which when the round starts.
- **Don't tell anyone your role.** Saboteurs blend in.

### The 3 stations
- **🔥 Grill Bot** — look at a patty, decide: **rare** or **done**?
- **🗑️ Trash Sorter** — look at an item, decide: **recycle**, **compost**, or **landfill**?
- **🔍 Review & Security** — look at recent labels submitted to ANY bot. If a label looks wrong, **flag it** — that removes it from the bot's training.

### How customers work
- Every 30 seconds, a customer walks up with an order.
- The bots try to fulfill the order based on what you've taught them.
- Right answer → happy customer 😋. Wrong answer → confused 😕 or angry 😡.
- Your team has a **Customer Satisfaction** meter at the top. Happy customers raise it; angry ones drop it.

### Saboteurs
- They give wrong labels on purpose.
- Their tell: they linger at a station a little longer than trainers do.
- They have a cooldown (~12 seconds) between sabotage attempts.

### Meetings
- Anyone can call up to **2 emergency meetings** per round.
- Discuss what you've seen. Vote to eject someone you think is sabotaging.
- Eject a saboteur → great. Eject a trainer → wasted vote, lost teammate.

### Winning
- **Crew wins** if Customer Satisfaction stays above **85%** when time runs out, OR if all saboteurs are voted out.
- **Saboteurs win** if Customer Satisfaction crashes to 0%, OR if time runs out with at least one saboteur still in.
- Round length: **6 minutes**.

---

## 3. Setup (1 minute)

> "Open `[live game URL]` on your laptop. Don't log in yet."

> "We need a host — [pick someone]. They'll click **Create a room**. Everyone else uses the **4-letter code** they get."

> "Pick a fun name. Don't use your real one — saboteurs can use that against you. Pick an avatar."

> "When everyone's in the lobby and you can see all of us, the host clicks **Start**."

---

## 4. Two priming questions (1 minute — ask before they hit Start)

Ask these out loud. **Don't tell them the answer.** Just let them sit.

> 1. "What do you think happens if you teach the robot wrong information?"
> 2. "If some people on your team are trying to mess things up, how would you figure out who they are? What clues might you look for?"

Pause. Let one or two students answer briefly. Then:

> "Alright. Find your laptop. Let's see how it goes."

---

## After the round

- Run **2 or 3 rounds total** so students experience being both a trainer and a saboteur.
- Between rounds, just say "let's go again" — no debriefing yet.
- After the last round, transition to the debrief deck (`02-debrief-outline.md`).

The debrief is where the AI literacy vocabulary gets attached. Don't rush it — give it 10–15 minutes.
